## Accessibility (Lighthouse Best Practices)

### Semantic HTML
- ✅ Proper HTML5 semantic elements (`<main>`, `<section>`, `<article>`, `<nav>`, `<footer>`)
- ✅ Proper heading hierarchy (h1 → h2 → h3)
- ✅ ARIA labels and roles where appropriate
- ✅ Skip to main content link

### ARIA Attributes
- ✅ `aria-labelledby` for sections
- ✅ `aria-label` for buttons and links
- ✅ `aria-hidden="true"` for decorative elements
- ✅ `aria-expanded` for collapsible content (FAQ)
- ✅ `aria-controls` for interactive elements
- ✅ `aria-required` for form fields
- ✅ `aria-invalid` for form validation
- ✅ `aria-live` for dynamic content
- ✅ `aria-busy` for loading states

### Keyboard Navigation
- ✅ All interactive elements are keyboard accessible
- ✅ Focus indicators on all focusable elements
- ✅ Tab order is logical
- ✅ Enter/Space key support for custom buttons

### Color Contrast
- ✅ WCAG AAA compliant contrast ratios
- ✅ Text: neutral-900 on neutral-50 (21:1)
- ✅ Primary buttons: white on primary-600 (7.5:1)
- ✅ Secondary buttons: neutral-900 on white (21:1)

### Images & Media
- ✅ All decorative images have `aria-hidden="true"`
- ✅ All icons used decoratively are marked with `aria-hidden`
- ✅ No missing alt text (no actual images used, only CSS/emojis)

### Forms
- ✅ All form fields have labels
- ✅ Required fields are marked with `aria-required`
- ✅ Form validation feedback with `aria-invalid`
- ✅ Error messages with `aria-live="assertive"`
- ✅ Success messages with `aria-live="polite"`

### Focus Management
- ✅ Visible focus indicators on all interactive elements
- ✅ Focus trap in modals (if any)
- ✅ Focus restoration after dynamic content updates
